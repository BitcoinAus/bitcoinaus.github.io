Max Kaye

Treasurer

As a cryptocurrency enthusiast, and a member of the Bitcoin community since 2010, Max has a vast array of experience dealing with *coin based technology; from running a NMC/BTC mining pool in 2011, to experimenting with trustless exchange in 2013.
Through the Bitcoin Association, he hopes to foster the natural social structures that could form around an economy based on Bitcoin.

Adam Poulton

Secretary

Adam is a self employed business owner and father of two living in Tasmania. Adam has a Degree in Applied Science from the University of Tasmania and has also had an interest in money and macro economics from an early age.
Since the global financial crisis in 2008, Adam has been studying economics, in particular the theories of the Austrian School. This has lead to his interest in Bitcoin, and his belief that this new currency can solve many of the worlds current economic problems. Adam is currently in Beta in a Bitcoin startup, designed to integrate Bitcoin into peoples daily lives, and hopes that in the future, his children will be more familiar with Bitcoin than with regular dollars.

Lauren Ryan

Board Member

Lauren graduated from a Commerce degree majoring in Finance and Commercial Law from Macquarie University. She has recently started working for one of Australia’s big four banks and has a deep seeded interest in how Bitcoin will be viewed from a financial and legal perspective. Educating consumers of the uses and advantages of Bitcoin is a strong interest which is a result of many years as a sports and Tennis coach. Financial freedom is arguable the main desire of “man” and the unique specifics of Bitcoin offer individuals the chance to take 100% control of their finances and their lives. This mere possibility is the driving force behind Lauren’s interest in Bitcoin and the reason why educating the general population is key for this new age way of conducting business to blossom in the current world economies.
